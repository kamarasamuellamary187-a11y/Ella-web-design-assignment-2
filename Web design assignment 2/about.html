About Leone Stars

Our History

The Sierra Leone national football team, affectionately known as the "Leone Stars," represents the proud nation of Sierra Leone in international football competitions. Established in 1960, the team is governed by the Sierra Leone Football Association (SLFA) and has been a symbol of national pride and unity for over six decades.

The team's nickname "Leone Stars" derives from the country's name, which means "Lion Mountains" in Portuguese, and reflects the strength, courage, and fighting spirit that characterizes both the team and the Sierra Leonean people.

Since their first international match on August 10, 1949, against Nigeria, the Leone Stars have competed against some of Africa's and the world's best teams, steadily building a reputation for resilience and competitive spirit.

Mission & Vision

Our Mission

To represent Sierra Leone with pride, integrity, and excellence in international football, while fostering national unity and inspiring the next generation of footballers through dedication, teamwork, and sportsmanship.

Our Vision

To become one of Africa's leading football nations, consistently competing at the highest level of continental and global competitions, while developing world-class talent and promoting the beautiful game throughout Sierra Leone.
